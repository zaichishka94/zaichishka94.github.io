Marie Curie

Marie Curie was born in Warsaw on 7 November
She graduated a gymnasium for girls on 12 June 1883
Unable to enroll in a regular institution of higher educatin because she was a woman, she became involved with the Flying University
Maria took a position as governess
In 1891 she left Poland for France
Marie hypothesized that the radiation was not the outcome of some interaction of molecules but must come from the atom itself. It was a first step to explanation of radiactivity
On 26 July 1895 Marie and Pierre were married
In 1897 Marie returned to Warsaw but she was denied a place Krakow University because she was a woman
Marie investigated of the magnetic properties of varius steels
She was studing of physics,cmemistry, and mathematics at the University of Paris
In July 1898 Curie and her husband published announcity the existence of an element which they named "polonium", in honour of her native Poland
On December 1898 the Curies announced the existence of a second element which they named "radium"
In December 1903 the Royal Swedish Academe of Sciences awarded Pierre Curie, Marie Curie, and Henri Becquerel the Nobel Prize in Physics. Marie was the first woman to be awarded a Nobel Prize
In her later years, she headed the Radium Institute
On July 1934 she died in Passy from aplastic anemia believed to have been contracted her long-term exposure to radiation
